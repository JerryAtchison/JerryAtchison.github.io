IT Leadership, Communication, and Change Management

Analyzed two workplace leadership scenarios involving adoption of new processes and selection of a team for an equipment-upgrade pilot. Evaluated leadership styles, employee resistance, stakeholder communication, consensus-building, and change-management strategies.

Project objective

Evaluate how personal strengths, leadership style, communication, stakeholder concerns, and change-management decisions affect technology teams.

What I produced

  • Applied Achiever, Learner, Focus, Intellection, and Futuristic strengths to a process-change scenario.
  • Compared charismatic and democratic leadership approaches for experienced developers and project coordinators.
  • Analyzed a stakeholder meeting involving competing team priorities, incomplete data, security concerns, morale, and schedule pressure.
  • Developed recommendations for communication, training, consensus-building, and implementation planning.

Key decisions

  • Match leadership style to the needs, experience, and concerns of the team.
  • Use evidence and structured stakeholder input when resolving competing priorities.
  • Use role-specific training and demonstrations to reduce resistance to new processes.
  • Set milestones, communication expectations, and feedback loops before implementation.
